Frequently Asked Questions

How this app works, what your role means, and how your data stays yours.

How does the role system work?

The role system uses your answers from the Find Your Role questionnaire to calculate which of the 15 civic roles best matches your skills, interests, and physical readiness.

Behind the scenes, your answers are converted into scores across several categories like Information Flow, Supplies & Resource Management, and Mutual Aid & Support. The highest‑scoring category determines your primary role, and that role is assigned a unique Role Number for quick reference.

What is a Role Number?

A Role Number is a unique ID that ties you to a specific civic role, like #1 Signal Runner or #10 Chronicler. It makes it easy to form balanced teams quickly—if you know you have a #4, #7, and #12 in a group, you instantly know you have supplies, logistics, and media covered.

What are categories and why do they matter?

Each role belongs to a broader Category, such as Information Flow or Logistics & Planning. Categories help you understand not just what you do, but how you fit into a larger civic system. When multiple people share their roles, you can quickly see which categories are strong and which are missing.

You can explore all categories and their roles on the Role Categories page.

Is my data private?

Yes. All data is stored locally on your device only. The app does not send your answers, runs, or badges to any server. There is no login, no cloud sync, and no hidden network calls.

You can confirm this by reviewing the Local Storage entry in the glossary and by exporting your data from the Export / Import Data page to see exactly what is stored.

Do I need internet to use this app?

You only need internet once—to load or install the app. After that, the app is designed to run fully offline using your browser’s PWA and Service Worker capabilities.

If you lose connection, the app will still open, your data will still be available, and core pages will still function. If a page can’t load, you will see the Offline or Error page instead of a blank screen.

What are badges and how do I earn them?

Badges are distinctions you earn by passing specific Math & Science Trials. Each test has its own badge—for example, passing the arithmetic trial might grant you a Arithmetic Adept badge, while passing the combined science trial might grant you a Systems Observer badge.

Badges appear on your Profile and help you signal extra skills when forming teams.

What is the 1‑Mile Run Tracker for?

The 1‑Mile Run Tracker is a simple way to measure your physical readiness over time. Your best mile time is stored locally and displayed on your Profile and Share Card.

The glossary entry for Run Tracker explains how times are stored, how history is logged, and how this data is used in the app.

What is the Share Card?

The Share Card is a visual summary of your role, role number, category, and best mile time. It’s designed to be saved as an image and shared in group chats, printed, or used in in‑person meetups.

The glossary entry for Share Card breaks down each field and how it’s generated.

Can I reset everything and start over?

Yes. On the Settings page, you can choose to reset all local data. This clears your role, run history, badges, and any other stored information. The glossary entry Data Reset explains exactly what is removed.

Where can I see definitions of all these terms?

The Glossary is the central reference for all key concepts in this app: Role Number, Category, Badge, Run Tracker, PWA, Service Worker, and more.
